Pulse v2 Direction

From dashboard skin to planning cockpit.

You were right: Pulse had the right direction, but it was missing the deeper UX spine. This version makes Pulse feel like a complete Foodiary product experience: daily budget, smart food entry, week readiness, neutral insights, and estimate context.

What was missing before

Product identityPulse needs to be more than purple cards. It should feel like a daily food planning command center.
Decision loopThe user should always know current estimate, next action, and what changed after adding food.
Search-first entryFatSecret + custom food source needs a first-class search, recent, favorites, and serving flow.
Week readinessThe Plan screen should reveal prepared, empty, future, and read-only days at a glance.
Useful insightsInsights should explain planning patterns in neutral language, not just show decorative charts.
Trust contextProfile should explain the estimated target clearly without making the app feel medical.

Pulse v2 UX spine

01
Today is the command centerRemaining estimate, progress, macros, meal timeline, and one obvious next action.
02
Add Food is search-firstRecent foods, provider trust, servings, and manual fallback live in one predictable flow.
03
Plan is week readinessEach day communicates whether it is planned, empty, today, future, or read-only.
04
Insights are neutral observationsPattern review, no shame language, no medical or nutrition claims.

Recommendation

Move forward with Pulse v2.

This is now a clearer design direction: energetic but still clean, data-forward but not overwhelming, and much closer to Foodiary’s actual product shape.
